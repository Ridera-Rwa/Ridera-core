# 1. Overview

The global gig and mobility economy has become one of the fastest-growing labor sectors in the world, powered by millions of riders, couriers, and on-demand workers who create billions of dollars in economic value every single day. Despite this massive contribution, gig workers remain financially invisible — their income is fragmented across platforms, unverifiable, and disconnected from modern digital finance.

**Ridera solves this problem by introducing a new category of Real-World Asset (RWA) tokenization called Real-World Work (RWW).**

Ridera converts real-world earnings from gig and mobility workers into a standardized, transparent, and verifiable on-chain asset known as **SRU (Standardized Revenue Unit)**. SRU is not a synthetic value or a reward point — it is backed by cryptographically verifiable proofs of work, representing the true economic output generated by individuals across 195+ countries.

At the core of Ridera is the **Ridera Oracle**, the infrastructure component responsible for securely processing and verifying earnings proofs from any gig or mobility platform (ride-hailing, food delivery, parcel logistics, fleet operations, etc.). These proofs are:

- **Authenticated** to confirm the source of income  
- **Normalized** to adjust for country-level income variations, working hours, and platform difficulty  
- **Converted into SRU**, a unified on-chain representation of real-world productivity  
- **Stored on-chain** to build a transparent financial identity for workers  

By transforming fragmented earnings into a standardized digital asset, Ridera creates the world’s first **income layer for the gig economy** — enabling fair rewards, reputation scoring, transparent performance metrics, and future financial products such as micro-credit, insurance, and income-backed tools.

Ridera is **not** a mobility app and does not replace existing gig platforms.  
Instead, it operates as the **global infrastructure layer** that sits above all platforms, connecting the real-world work economy to the blockchain in a decentralized, tamper-proof, and programmable format.

Through this architecture, Ridera unlocks a new financial paradigm where human labor becomes a verifiable asset — enabling workers, platforms, and investors to participate in a sustainable, transparent, and globally scalable ecosystem.

Ridera represents a shift from traditional asset-backed RWAs (real estate, treasuries) to a new model where **human productivity itself becomes a tokenizable, measurable, and economically valuable on-chain asset**.

This is the future of work-based finance — and Ridera is building the foundational layer for it.

# 5. How Ridera Works

Ridera converts daily gig-worker earnings into SRU (Standardized Revenue Units) through a structured, verifiable, and fully on-chain workflow.  
This system ensures transparency, fairness, and real economic backing for all SRU generated.

Below is the complete end-to-end operational flow.


## 5.1 Earnings Upload (Worker → Ridera)

Each worker uploads their daily earnings data to the Ridera website.  
This can include:

- Screenshots of earnings  
- Platform-generated income summaries  
- Trip/delivery reports  
- Total hours worked  
- Platform-specific performance data  

All data submitted on the same day forms the **input for one cycle**.

**1 Day = 1 Cycle**


## 5.2 Ridera Oracle Verification

Once earnings are uploaded, the **Ridera Oracle** performs multi-layer authentication:

- **Validates the data source**  
- **Checks timestamps**  
- **Detects edits or manipulation**  
- **Matches platform formats**  
- **Confirms income values with strict rules**  
- **Runs anti-fraud filters**  

Only fully verified proofs are passed to the next stage.

Any invalid or suspicious data is rejected.


## 5.3 Cycle Formation (Daily Work Cycle)

Ridera groups all verified earnings submitted on a given day into one **Cycle**.

A Cycle includes:

- Day’s total earnings  
- Hours worked  
- Platform breakdown  
- Region and difficulty factors  
- Worker productivity score  

Every worker generates **one cycle per day**, regardless of earnings amount.


## 5.4 SRU Generation (Standardized Revenue Unit)

After the cycle is confirmed, Ridera calculates the SRU output for that day.

SRU is derived from:

- **Earnings amount**  
- **Working hours**  
- **Platform difficulty index**  
- **Country economic scaling**  
- **Work efficiency metrics**

This produces a **single SRU value per daily cycle**, representing the worker’s real economic contribution in a standardized, global format.


## 5.5 Proof Registry (On-Chain Storage)

Once SRU is calculated, Ridera stores the cycle data in the **Proof Registry**.

The registry includes:

- **SRU amount**  
- **Merkle Root** of all cycle data  
- **Timestamp**  
- **Worker identifier hash**  

This creates a **permanent, tamper-proof record** of each worker’s daily verified work.

The Proof Registry is the trust layer for the entire system.


## 5.6 Yield Vault (Reads SRU Proofs → Generates Rewards)

The **Yield Vault** automatically reads the stored cycle data from the Proof Registry every day.

It determines:

- Total SRU generated by all workers  
- Each worker’s SRU share  
- Total RDR rewards available for that day  
- Reward distribution ratios  

The Yield Vault does **not** hold worker income.  
It only uses **SRU output** as the metric to compute protocol-level token emissions.


## 5.7 Staking Contract (Final RDR Distribution)

Once the Yield Vault calculates the daily reward amounts, it sends reward instructions to the **Staking Contract**.

The Staking Contract then distributes:

- **Daily RDR rewards to stakers**  
- Based on:
  - Their staked amount  
  - Their share of the reward pool  
  - The SRU-generated emissions for the day  

Workers do not stake to earn SRU —  
**SRU drives protocol emissions → emissions reward the stakers.**


## 5.8 End-to-End Daily Cycle Summary

1. **Worker uploads daily earnings**  
2. **Ridera Oracle verifies authenticity**  
3. **A Cycle is created (1 day = 1 cycle)**  
4. **SRU is generated for that cycle**  
5. **Cycle data stored on-chain in Proof Registry (Merkle Root + timestamp)**  
6. **Yield Vault reads SRU proofs and calculates daily rewards**  
7. **Staking Contract pays out RDR rewards to stakers**  

This creates a closed-loop, trustless system where **real-world work → standardized data → on-chain proof → rewards**.

# 6. SRU Engine

The SRU Engine is Ridera’s core computational layer responsible for transforming real-world gig-worker earnings into a standardized on-chain measurement called **SRU (Standardized Revenue Unit)**.  
SRU enables fair comparison of worker productivity across different countries, platforms, and working conditions by applying normalization factors that reflect real economic variations.

The SRU Engine operates after earnings data has been verified by the Ridera Oracle and assigned to a daily cycle.


## 6.1 Purpose of SRU Standardization

Real-world earnings vary significantly due to:

- Geographic income differences  
- Platform-specific payout structures  
- Worker effort and working hours  
- Local demand and labor economics  

Without standardization, raw earnings cannot be used to calculate fair incentives or represent comparable economic activity.  
SRU resolves this by converting earnings into a single, globally consistent metric that reflects true work contribution.


## 6.2 Country Weightage

Each country has a different average mobility income, cost of living, and earning potential.  
To ensure fair comparison, the SRU Engine assigns a **country weight multiplier**.

Examples (illustrative):

- India — 1.00x  
- Vietnam — 0.90x  
- Philippines — 0.95x  
- UAE — 1.40x  
- USA — 1.80x  

This correction ensures that equivalent effort in two regions produces comparable SRU output.


## 6.3 Platform Weightage

Gig platforms differ in compensation, delivery density, surge behavior, and earning stability.  
The SRU Engine adjusts for these variations using **platform weight multipliers**.

Examples (illustrative):

- Zomato — 1.00x  
- Swiggy — 0.95x  
- Uber Eats — 1.20x  
- Grab — 1.10x  

This ensures platform-specific payout structures do not distort worker value.


## 6.4 Category / Work-Type Weightage

Workers operate with different levels of commitment and opportunity.  
The SRU Engine accounts for effort variations via **category multipliers**.

Examples (illustrative):

- Full-time rider — 1.20x  
- Part-time rider — 1.00x  
- Peak-hours rider — 1.10x  
- Low-traffic region — 0.90x  

These multipliers adjust SRU output based on worker behavior and operating conditions.


## 6.5 SRU Calculation Formula

After applying all normalization factors, SRU is calculated as:

**SRU = (Earnings_in_USD × CountryWeight × PlatformWeight × CategoryWeight)**

The formula produces a standardized representation of the worker’s daily productivity.


## 6.6 Cycle Formation (Daily Output)

Ridera processes earnings on a daily basis.

- One day of verified earnings = **One Cycle**  
- A cycle contains all normalized earnings (SRU) generated by a worker on that date  
- Each cycle has a unique cycleId  

Cycles allow the protocol to process and distribute rewards in discrete, consistent periods.


## 6.7 Merkle Root Generation and Proof Registry Storage

To optimize on-chain efficiency, Ridera stores only aggregated cycle proofs on-chain.

For each cycle, the SRU Engine generates:

- A **Merkle Tree** of all individual worker SRU values  
- A **Merkle Root** representing the combined dataset  
- A **timestamp** marking the cycle finalization  
- The **total SRU** generated during that cycle

The **Proof Registry** stores:

- cycleId  
- totalSRU  
- merkleRoot  
- timestamp  

This structure ensures immutability, data integrity, and verifiable proof-of-work output without storing all worker data on-chain.


## 6.8 Output of the SRU Engine

At the end of each cycle, the SRU Engine delivers the following:

- Individual SRU values (stored off-chain)  
- Total SRU generated for the cycle  
- Merkle Root of all worker submissions  
- A finalized dataset that the Yield Vault uses to compute daily RDR emissions  

The SRU output serves as the economic foundation for reward distribution across the ecosystem.

The system becomes fully automated once Ridera integrates with verified platform APIs.

Future versions of the SRU Engine will include:

# 8. Proof Registry

The Proof Registry is Ridera’s on-chain storage layer for verified gig-worker productivity.  
It provides a permanent, tamper-proof, and cryptographically secure record of all SRU-generating work cycles.  
Instead of storing raw data on-chain, Ridera uses a Merkle-based structure to ensure efficiency, security, and full auditability.

The Proof Registry serves as the single source of truth for the Yield Vault and all downstream integrations.


## 8.1 Purpose of the Proof Registry

The Proof Registry exists to:

- Record verified daily cycles  
- Store Merkle Roots representing worker submissions  
- Preserve a decentralized proof of economic activity  
- Enable reward calculation based on standardized work outputs  
- Ensure transparency without exposing sensitive worker information  

This allows Ridera to authenticate real-world productivity in a trustless, on-chain manner.


## 8.2 Why Merkle Trees Are Used

Storing individual earnings or SRU values for every worker directly on-chain is expensive and inefficient.  
To solve this, Ridera uses a Merkle Tree:

- Each worker’s cycle SRU forms a **leaf node**  
- Leaves are recursively hashed  
- A single **Merkle Root** represents the entire dataset  

Advantages:

- Minimal on-chain storage  
- Fast verification  
- Cryptographic integrity  
- Immutable history  

This design ensures the Proof Registry can scale to millions of daily submissions.


## 8.3 Structure of a Proof Registry Entry

Each daily cycle generates a single entry in the Proof Registry.

A registry entry contains:

- **cycleId** — Unique identifier for the cycle  
- **totalSRU** — Total SRU generated by all workers in that cycle  
- **merkleRoot** — Hash root representing all individual SRU values  
- **timestamp** — Block time at cycle finalization  

Only the root and summary values are stored on-chain.  
Individual SRU values remain off-chain but can always be verified against the Merkle tree.


## 8.4 Data Flow to the Proof Registry

The pipeline follows this order:

1. Worker submits earnings data  
2. Ridera Oracle verifies all proofs  
3. SRU Engine normalizes the data and calculates SRU values  
4. A Merkle Tree is constructed from all SRU outputs  
5. Registry entry is generated  
6. Merkle Root + total SRU + timestamp stored on-chain  

This ensures a transparent and consistent validation process.


## 8.5 Integrity and Verification

Any stakeholder (worker, auditor, partner platform, or contract) can verify a worker’s SRU data by:

- Retrieving the Merkle Root for the cycle  
- Generating a Merkle Proof from off-chain data  
- Validating that the proof matches the stored root  

This allows trustless verification without exposing personal data.


## 8.6 Interaction with the Yield Vault

The Proof Registry is the only source the Yield Vault reads to calculate daily emissions.

For each cycle, the Yield Vault uses:

- totalSRU to determine reward emissions  
- merkleRoot to validate worker-level distribution proofs  
- timestamp to maintain chronological order  

The registry ensures that no rewards are issued for unverified or manipulated data.


## 8.7 Security Considerations

The Proof Registry ensures system integrity through:

- Immutable on-chain storage  
- Cryptographic hashing  
- Replay protection  
- Cycle-level idempotency  
- Strict ordering of entries  

Any attempt to alter past productivity records becomes computationally infeasible.


## 8.8 Future Enhancements

Future versions of the Proof Registry will support:

- Cross-cycle analytics  
- Zero-knowledge proof extensions  
- Multi-chain registry replication  
- Enhanced data compression  
- Real-time registry indexing for partner platforms  

These upgrades will extend the Proof Registry into a universal proof-of-labor infrastructure layer for the global gig economy.

# 9. Yield Vault

The Yield Vault is Ridera’s reward computation engine responsible for transforming verified real-world productivity (represented as SRU) into protocol-level token emissions.  
It reads finalized cycle data from the Proof Registry and calculates the daily RDR rewards that flow into the staking contract.

The Yield Vault does not store or manage worker income.  
Its role is limited to interpreting SRU outputs and determining fair, transparent, and economically aligned reward distributions.

## 9.1 Purpose of the Yield Vault

The Yield Vault provides a decentralized, rules-based mechanism to:

- Convert totalSRU into daily RDR emissions  
- Calculate each worker’s reward share based on SRU  
- Allocate emission amounts to the Staking Contract  
- Prevent reward issuance for unverified data  
- Maintain a consistent reward cycle tied to real-world activity  

It ensures that the protocol distributes tokens only when real economic output is recorded.


## 9.2 Input Source: Proof Registry

For every daily work cycle, the Yield Vault reads the following data from the Proof Registry:

- **totalSRU** — Aggregate worker productivity for the cycle  
- **merkleRoot** — Proof of all individual SRU values  
- **timestamp** — Cycle completion time  
- **cycleId** — Unique cycle reference  

The vault never reads raw earnings data; it relies solely on validated SRU computations.


## 9.3 Dynamic Emission Model

The Yield Vault applies Ridera’s Dynamic Emission Curve Model (DECM) to convert verified real-world productivity (measured as totalSRU per cycle) into controlled, sustainable RDR token emissions.  
This model ensures that rewards scale with ecosystem activity while maintaining strict token-supply discipline.

The emission curve increases smoothly as totalSRU grows, but it never exceeds the protocol’s defined maximum emission capacity.


### 9.2.1 Emission Formula

The Yield Vault computes the protocol’s daily RDR emission using the following function:

dailyEmission = maxDailyEmission × (1 - e^( - totalSRU / SRUTarget ))


Where:

- **maxDailyEmission** — The maximum number of RDR tokens that can be emitted per cycle.  
- **SRUTarget** — The standardized productivity level at which the emission curve reaches its optimal operational range.  
- **totalSRU** — The aggregate SRU generated across all verified workers for that cycle.

This function ensures a continuously increasing reward response to real productivity, while the exponential decay term prevents excessive inflation as SRU grows.


### 9.2.2 Emission Curve Behavior

The emission curve exhibits the following characteristics:

- **Low SRU → Low Emission:**  
  When totalSRU is below SRUTarget, emissions grow proportionally and remain conservative.

- **Optimal SRU → Balanced Emission:**  
  At around SRUTarget, the emission output reaches the system’s efficient operating zone.

- **High SRU → Asymptotic Emission:**  
  As totalSRU becomes very large, emission approaches maxDailyEmission but never exceeds it.

This ensures continuity, predictability, and inflation-resistant token issuance.


### 9.2.3 Emission Output to the Staking Contract

After computing `dailyEmission`, the Yield Vault forwards this amount to the Staking Contract.

The Staking Contract distributes rewards proportionally:

stakerReward = (userStake / totalStaked) × dailyEmission

This creates a fair and transparent distribution model tied directly to verifiable real-world output.

# 12. Economic Model

- Daily maximum emission caps  
- Emission decay schedule (optional)

This ensures emissions scale proportionally with actual economic activity.

# 18. Glossary

This glossary provides clear definitions for core concepts, components, and terminology used throughout the Ridera protocol.  
It serves as a reference for readers, developers, partners, and contributors working with Ridera’s Real-World Work (RWW) infrastructure.


## 18.1 Standardized Revenue Unit (SRU)

SRU is Ridera’s core productivity metric.  
It represents a standardized, normalized, and verified measure of gig-worker earnings across countries, platforms, and work conditions.

SRU ensures fair comparison of work value globally.


## 18.2 Cycle

A Cycle is a 24-hour collection of verified earnings for a worker.  
Each cycle generates:

- Individual SRU values  
- A Merkle Root  
- A timestamp  
- A totalSRU entry  

Cycles form the basis for reward computation and verifiable on-chain proof.


## 18.3 Ridera Oracle

The verification module that authenticates gig-worker earnings.  
It performs:

- Authenticity checks  
- Fraud scanning  
- Timestamp and platform verification  
- Data normalization preparation  

Only verified data enters the SRU Engine.


## 18.4 SRU Engine

The standardization layer that converts verified earnings into SRU using:

- Country Weightage  
- Platform Weightage  
- Category/Work-Type Weightage  

It produces final SRU values and prepares Merkle Tree inputs.


## 18.5 Proof Registry

The on-chain storage layer that records:

- Merkle Root for each cycle  
- totalSRU  
- timestamp  
- cycleId  

It acts as the immutable source of truth for all verified work cycles.


## 18.6 Yield Vault

The reward computation engine that:

- Reads cycle data from the Proof Registry  
- Computes daily RDR emissions  
- Sends reward instructions to the staking system  

It transforms SRU activity into protocol-level token incentives.


## 18.7 Staking Contract

The module responsible for:

- Accepting staked RDR  
- Receiving daily emission parameters  
- Distributing rewards proportionally to stakers  

It aligns long-term participants with protocol growth.


## 18.8 RDR Token

The native token of Ridera, used for:

- Staking rewards  
- Governance (future)  
- Platform incentives  
- DeFi integrations  
- Partner ecosystem tooling  

RDR connects economic activity, governance, and incentives across the protocol.


## 18.9 Merkle Root

The cryptographic hash representing all SRU values in a cycle.  
It allows:

- Efficient on-chain storage  
- Proof-of-inclusion verification  
- Immutable audit trails  

It is a core element of Ridera’s verifiable work-proof architecture.


## 18.10 Real-World Work (RWW)

A new RWA (Real-World Asset) category introduced by Ridera.  
RWW tokenizes **human productivity** instead of physical assets.

Work becomes a measurable, verifiable, and valuable digital asset.


## 18.11 Earnings Proof

Any verifiable data submitted by a worker that reflects real economic activity.  
Examples include:

- Platform screenshots  
- Delivery logs  
- Ride histories  
- Income summaries  

These proofs are validated by the Ridera Oracle.


## 18.12 Weight Multipliers

Normalization factors applied by the SRU Engine:

- Country Weightage  
- Platform Weightage  
- Category/Work-Type Weightage  

These ensure fair representation of work across different conditions.


## 18.13 Base Chain

The blockchain where Ridera is deployed.  
Base provides:

- Low fees  
- High throughput  
- Coinbase-backed infrastructure  
- Ideal environment for micro-cycle RWA operations  

It serves as the primary settlement layer for Ridera.


## 18.14 Real-World Asset (RWA)

A blockchain category where real-world economic value is represented digitally.  
Ridera is an RWA protocol focused on **work**, not real estate or commodities.


## 18.15 Worker Portal

The interface workers use to:

- Upload earnings  
- Track cycle validation  
- View SRU outputs  
- Understand proof and reward flow  

It is the entry point for gig workers into the Ridera ecosystem.


## 18.16 Emission

The daily release of RDR tokens distributed to stakers.  
Emissions are tied directly to totalSRU and verified economic activity.


## 18.17 TotalSRU

The aggregate SRU generated by all workers in a cycle.  
It is used by the Yield Vault to determine daily RDR emission amounts.

# 19. Full Protocol Summary

Ridera is a decentralized Real-World Work (RWW) infrastructure protocol that transforms gig-worker earnings into standardized, verifiable, and on-chain digital assets.  
It bridges real economic activity—riders, delivery workers, and mobility operators—with blockchain infrastructure through a secure, modular, and auditable architecture.

The protocol enables global income standardization, transparent proof-of-work cycles, and fair token incentives backed by real human productivity.


# 19.1 Core Idea

Ridera solves one fundamental problem:

**Gig workers produce billions in economic value every day, but none of it is standardized, verifiable, or usable in modern financial systems.**

Ridera captures this value using:

- Verified earnings  
- Normalized productivity metrics  
- Cryptographic proof storage  
- On-chain transparency  
- Real-work-backed reward models  

It creates the world’s first **work-based RWA asset class**.


# 19.2 Protocol Flow Summary

The Ridera protocol operates sequentially through five major components:

### **1. Earnings Submission**
Workers upload their daily platform earnings into the Ridera Portal.

### **2. Verification (Ridera Oracle)**
Proofs are validated for authenticity, timestamps, platform consistency, and manipulation.

### **3. Standardization (SRU Engine)**
Verified earnings are converted into SRU using:
- Country weight  
- Platform weight  
- Category/work-type weight  

One day = one standardized **Cycle**.

### **4. Proof Storage (Proof Registry)**
Each cycle is compressed into a:
- Merkle Root  
- totalSRU  
- timestamp  
- cycleId  

These are permanently stored on-chain.

### **5. Reward Computation (Yield Vault → Staking)**
The Yield Vault reads cycle data, computes daily emissions, and the Staking Contract distributes RDR rewards to stakers.

This creates a closed-loop, tamper-proof system that ties **real-world work → on-chain value → reward emission**.

# 20. User Journey

The Ridera User Journey outlines how individuals interact with the protocol, including gig workers who generate SRU through verified earnings and stakers who support the network and receive RDR rewards.  
This section provides a step-by-step view of how Ridera functions from a user perspective.


# 20.1 Worker Journey (Gig Worker)

The worker journey represents how a gig worker contributes real-world productivity to the Ridera protocol and builds verifiable work identity through SRU cycles.


## 20.1.1 Step 1 — Account Registration
The worker creates an account on the Ridera platform, providing:
- Basic profile information  
- Country and region  
- Preferred gig platforms  

This initializes the worker’s identity within the protocol.


## 20.1.2 Step 2 — Earnings Submission
Each day, the worker uploads proof of earnings via:
- Screenshots  
- Income reports  
- Delivery/trip logs  
- Platform summaries  

In future phases:
- Automatic screenshot capture  
- API integrations with gig platforms  
will eliminate manual input.


## 20.1.3 Step 3 — Oracle Verification
The Ridera Oracle validates all submitted evidence through:
- Authenticity checks  
- Metadata screening  
- Timestamp verification  
- Platform-specific pattern analysis  
- Tampering and manipulation detection  

Only verified proofs move forward.


## 20.1.4 Step 4 — Cycle Creation
Each 24-hour period forms a **Cycle**.  
For every cycle, the protocol groups:
- Worker earnings  
- Work hours  
- Platform type  
- Country region  
- Work category  

This cycle becomes a standardized representation of the worker’s daily productivity.


## 20.1.5 Step 5 — SRU Generation
The SRU Engine computes the worker’s standardized output using:
- Country Weight  
- Platform Weight  
- Category Weight  

**One SRU value is produced for each worker per cycle.**

This creates a fair, globally comparable productivity score.


## 20.1.6 Step 6 — On-Chain Proof Storage
The worker’s SRU value becomes part of a Merkle Tree, which generates:
- A Merkle Root  
- totalSRU for the cycle  
- A timestamp  

The Proof Registry stores these on-chain, creating permanent proof of verified work.



## 20.1.7 Step 7 — Building Work Identity
Over time, each worker accumulates:
- A history of verified cycles  
- A growing work identity score  
- Proof-backed reputation  

This forms the foundation for future:
- Credit systems  
- Work-score-based insurance  
- Gig platform incentives  
- Financial inclusion tools  


# 20.2 Staker Journey (RDR Token Holder)

Stakers support Ridera’s long-term stability and receive daily RDR rewards tied to real-world productivity.


## 20.2.1 Step 1 — Staking RDR
A staker deposits RDR into the staking contract on Base.  
This locks their tokens and registers them for reward eligibility.


## 20.2.2 Step 2 — Yield Vault Reads SRU Data
Each day:
- The Yield Vault reads the cycle's totalSRU from the Proof Registry  
- Computes daily RDR emissions  
- Sends reward allocation instructions to the staking contract  


## 20.2.3 Step 3 — Reward Distribution
The staking contract distributes rewards proportionally:

**stakerReward = (stakerStake / totalStaked) × dailyEmission**

Stakers receive rewards derived from the ecosystem’s real-work productivity.


## 20.2.4 Step 4 — Claiming Rewards
Stakers can claim RDR at any time through:
- Ridera dashboard  
- On-chain contract interaction  

Rewards accumulate daily based on protocol activity.


## 20.2.5 Step 5 — Long-Term Participation
Stakers may:
- Increase stake  
- Lock tokens for enhanced rewards (future)  
- Participate in governance (future)  
- Support ecosystem partnerships  

This strengthens Ridera’s economic stability.


# 20.3 Summary: Combined User Experience

### **Workers**
Generate SRU → Build proof-of-work identity → Strengthen protocol → Expand RWW dataset.

### **Stakers**
Stake RDR → Earn rewards → Support protocol → Benefit from global real-work activity.

Together, both user groups drive Ridera’s unique RWA model where **human productivity becomes a tokenized, verifiable, and incentivized digital asset class.**
