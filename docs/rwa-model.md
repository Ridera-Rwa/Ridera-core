# Ridera RWA Model  
### Mobility Revenue → Tokenized Real-World Yield  
### Ridera Protocol — Global Real-World Asset Framework

---

# 1. Overview

Ridera introduces a new class of Real-World Assets (RWA) built on top of one of the world’s largest and most stable economic systems: the global mobility economy.

Traditional RWA categories focus on:
- real estate  
- treasuries  
- commodities  
- business receivables  

These models rely on centralized institutions, financial contracts, and predictable but low-yield structures.

Ridera instead focuses on an **entirely new global RWA category**:

## Mobility Yield RWA (MY-RWA)
Real-world mobility revenue generated by:
- ride-hailing drivers  
- delivery couriers  
- logistics fleets  
- multi-platform mobility workers  

This revenue is verified, standardized, and transformed into **on-chain yield** through the Ridera protocol.

This document outlines the complete RWA model.

---

# 2. Why Mobility is the Ideal RWA Base Layer

The global mobility economy has unique qualities that make it stronger than most RWA categories.

## 2.1 High Frequency & High Volume
Millions of mobility tasks occur every hour:
- trips  
- deliveries  
- courier routes  
- parcel runs  

This creates a **continuous revenue stream**—not monthly, not weekly, but **daily**.

## 2.2 Essential Global Activity
Mobility is:
- non-speculative  
- essential to daily life  
- tied to food, transportation, commerce  
- resistant to economic downturns  
- active in every geographic market  

## 2.3 Labor-Driven, Not Market-Driven
Revenue is earned through:
- human effort  
- completed tasks  
- route execution  

Not through:
- price speculation  
- interest rates  
- market volatility  
- synthetic financial structures  

This makes it one of the **purest forms of real yield**.

## 2.4 Geographic Risk Diversification
Unlike property or national treasuries, mobility income is distributed across:
- APAC  
- LATAM  
- MENA  
- EU  
- Africa  
- North America  

A global worker base creates natural risk diversification.

# 3. Ridera’s RWA Flow

Ridera converts global mobility income into an on-chain RWA lifecycle using a 4-step model.

---

# 3.1 Step 1 — Revenue Collection

Drivers, couriers, and fleets generate real-world income through:
- Uber, Lyft, Bolt, Grab  
- DoorDash, Deliveroo, iFood, Foodpanda  
- Rappi, Gojek, Careem  
- regional mobility platforms  

This income appears in:
- screenshots  
- daily payout statements  
- weekly summaries  
- platform receipts  

---

# 3.2 Step 2 — Verification (G-FRP + Ridera Oracle)

All revenue enters the verification layer:
1. Data extraction (OCR + parsing)
2. Timestamp validation  
3. Platform consistency checks  
4. Region-level payout range checks  
5. Image anomaly detection  
6. Duplicate submission detection  
7. Reputation scoring  
8. Validator audits  

Once verified, earnings become standardized in the form of:

```
Standardized Revenue Units (SRU)
```

---

# 3.3 Step 3 — Aggregation (Yield Vault)

SRUs from around the world flow into the global Ridera Yield Vault.

This vault:
- aggregates daily SRUs  
- models yield ratios  
- calculates rewards for stakers  
- publishes transparency logs  

Yield is based on real-world performance:
- more verified revenue = higher yield  
- more regions = better stability  
- more fleets = deeper global coverage  

---

# 3.4 Step 4 — Yield Distribution

$RDR stakers receive proportional yield based on:
- global SRU volume  
- region weighting  
- total staked supply  
- validator participation  

Yield cycles:
- 24 hours (testnet)  
- 12 hours (mainnet v1)  
- 1-hour cycles (future upgrades)  

---

# 4. The RWA Asset: Standardized Revenue Units (SRU)

The SRU is the fundamental asset in the Ridera system.

### SRU represents:
- verified mobility revenue  
- cross-platform normalized earnings  
- region-adjusted payout values  
- consistent and predictable income  

### SRU ensures:
- earnings from a driver in Brazil and a driver in India can be compared  
- earnings from two different platforms (e.g., Uber + DoorDash) can be merged  
- multi-region revenue is standardized into a unified value  

SRU = the backbone of Ridera’s RWA tokenization.

---

# 5. Region Weighting Model

Because payout levels differ globally, Ridera uses a **region-weighted SRU model**:

### High-income regions
- North America  
- Middle East  
- Western Europe  

### Medium-income regions
- LATAM  
- Southeast Asia  

### Low-income but high-volume regions
- India  
- Philippines  
- Egypt  
- Indonesia  

Each region has:
- minimum payout thresholds  
- maximum expected range  
- surge patterns  
- task density profiles  

Region weighting prevents:
- distortions  
- extreme differences  
- unfair advantage  
- arbitrage exploitation

# 10. Compliance Philosophy

Ridera avoids:
- custody of fleet earnings  
- holding rider payments  
- directly touching platform payouts  

Instead, Ridera verifies proof-of-income and tokenizes the **verified value**, not the income itself.

This ensures:
- global neutrality  
- reduced regulatory risk  
- clear RWA positioning  
- open global participation
