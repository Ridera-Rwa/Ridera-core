## üõ° Disclosure Policy

We follow responsible disclosure standards:

- Do *NOT* publicly disclose any vulnerability before we resolve it.  
- Do *NOT* exploit or damage the protocol or other users.  
- Do *NOT* access data you are not authorized to access.  
- Do *NOT* perform Denial-of-Service attacks or spamming.  
- You may perform *testing on beta/testnet deployments only*, not mainnet.

Following these guidelines ensures you remain eligible for recognition and rewards.

## üîç Eligible Vulnerability Areas

We accept reports for issues in:

### *Smart Contracts*
- Reentrancy  
- Overflow/Underflow  
- Unauthorized access  
- Logic manipulation  
- Incorrect reward distribution  
- Merkle root verification issues  
- Economic vulnerabilities  
- Gas griefing or DoS  

### *Web App (Frontend)*
- Authentication issues  
- Session hijacking  
- Privilege escalation  
- XSS, CSRF, or SSRF  
- Insecure direct object access  

### *API & Backend*
- Leaks of sensitive data  
- Improper authorization checks  
- Rate-limiting bypass  
- Broken access control  

---

## ‚ö† Non-Eligible Reports

We do NOT consider the following as valid vulnerabilities:

- Outdated browser issues  
- Self-XSS  
- Low-impact UI bugs  
- Typos  
- Issues affecting old or deprecated beta contracts  
- Missing best-practices that do not impact security

## üîÑ Vulnerability Handling Process

1. Researcher submits vulnerability report.  
2. Ridera team acknowledges within *48 hours*.  
3. Issue is validated internally.  
4. Fix is prepared and deployed on testnet/beta.  
5. Researcher is notified once fixed.  
6. (Optional) Reward or acknowledgment is issued.  
7. Issue is documented internally for future audits.
